# Quick 1v1 Game Ideas — Viral Filter

## Design principles
- Understandable in 5 seconds, no tutorial
- Peak tension heartbeat moment
- Produces a shareable outcome (screenshot-worthy, surprising, funny, or relatable)
- Sub-90s rounds, sub-3min matches
- One clear winner/loser → loser picks VR locations → winner chooses → chat or lobby

---

## Nerve — "Who blinks first"

Games where the tension comes from holding out one more millisecond.

### 1. The Button
Shared timer ticks 0→10s. Both must press stop. Closest to exactly 10s **without going over** wins. The final ms are agony. The result is a number — shareable to prove your nerves of steel.

- Round: ~12s | Build: Very Low
- *Share hook: "I hit 9.97s — can you beat it?"*

### 2. Chicken
Bar fills 0→100%. Both hold. First to release loses. Every ms past 80% is a battle. The result screen shows your "breaking point" percentage.

- Round: 8-30s | Build: Very Low
- *Share hook: "I held to 97% before flinching. You?"*

### 3. Roulette
6 chambers, 1 bullet. Alternating: fire at opponent or pass. Fire and hit → win. Fire and miss → opponent's turn. Chamber 6 is death. The close calls (chamber 5) are the shareable moment.

- Round: ~30s | Build: Low
- *Share hook: "I fired on chamber 5 and LIVED. The opponent... didn't."*

### 4. Flip
9 cards (1-9) face down. Alternating flips. Flip higher than last → continue your turn. Flip lower → turn ends. Flip the 9 → YOU LOSE INSTANTLY. Late-game with 2 cards left and the 9 hiding is excruciating. The moment the 9 appears is pure drama.

- Round: ~30s | Build: Low
- *Share hook: screenshot of 9 face up on opponent's turn*

### 5. Bomb
5×5 grid, one bomb. Alternating flips. Safe tiles show mine-count. Last 2 tiles = one is the bomb, opponent's turn. The anticipation is brutal.

- Round: ~45s | Build: Medium
- *Share hook: "2 tiles left. Opponent flipped the bomb on their turn. I've never felt more alive."*

### 6. Dice Fate
A single die tumbles in super slow-motion. Both bet high (4-6) or low (1-3). Correct = 1pt. First to 5 wins. The die settles on 3 — you bet high. SO CLOSE. The animation is the star — physics-rendered, weighty, dramatic.

- Round: ~5s/rd | Build: Very Low
- *Share hook: the slow-motion die GIF — "THIS was the moment I lost"*

## Reflex — "Fastest wins"

### 7. Quick Draw
Countdown: 3... 2... 1... DRAW. First to tap fire wins. Tap before the signal → disqualified. The 2.9s moment is agonizing. Best of 5. The reaction face when someone flinches early is gold.

- Round: ~5s/rd | Build: Very Low
- *Share hook: video of the draw — who flinched?*

### 8. Bell Ringer
Category appears ("A fruit", "A capital city"). Both type an answer and slam their bell. First to ring with a VALID answer wins. Wrong answer = opponent wins. Categories escalate progressively. The bell slam animation — brass tilting, clanging — makes every answer feel monumental.

- Round: ~10s/rd | Build: Low
- *Share hook: "I blanked on 'something that floats' and opponent rang in with 'duck' before I could think"*

## Psychology — "Who can read who"

### 9. Simul-Pick
Both secretly pick one of three symbols. Reveal simultaneously. Each beats one, loses to one. First to 3pts wins. The meta-game of patterns and tells across 7 rounds is the real game.

- Round: ~8s/rd | Build: Very Low
- *Share hook: the 7-round pattern graph — "I knew they'd pick rock on round 5"*

### 10. Compass
Both secretly choose N, S, E, W. A hidden "true north" is revealed. Closest wins. True north shifts in a pattern across 7 rounds — you're decoding both the pattern AND the opponent's read on the pattern. The compass needle spinning and settling is beautiful.

- Round: ~6s/rd | Build: Very Low
- *Share hook: the spinning compass GIF — hypnotic, shareable*

### 11. Sabotage
Both decide: **build** or **sabotage**. Both build → tower grows. Both sabotage → tower shrinks. One builds, one sabotages → tower collapses (saboteur wins). Your last 3 choices visible to opponent as a blurry timeline. First to 3 wins. The tower collapse physics are the money shot.

- Round: ~6s/rd | Build: Very Low
- *Share hook: the tower collapse animation — physics, debris, drama*

### 12. The Offering
Both have 3 tokens with attributes (color, shape, material). A shrine desires something ("round", "red", "shiny"). You offer a token. The shrine judges. Best match wins. **BUT**: same token = both rejected. So you're predicting their pick while maximizing your score. Ornate shrine, incense smoke, glowing verdict.

- Round: ~10s/rd | Build: Medium
- *Share hook: "We BOTH offered the red token. The shrine rejected us both. Awkward."*

## Social / Creative — "Makes something to talk about"

Games where the outcome isn't just a winner — it's shared content that sparks conversation.

### 13. Which One
This-or-that: "Beach or Mountains?" "Dogs or Cats?" Both pick. Reveal simultaneously. Match = shared joy (1pt each). Mismatch = discovery (0pts). First to 5pts wins. Bonus round: "Guess their STRONGEST opinion." The cards are beautifully illustrated, the reveal is warm.

- Round: ~5s/rd | Build: Very Low
- *Share hook: "We matched on 6/7. Soulmates?" + side-by-side card match graphic*

### 14. Story Dice
3 random icons appear. Both have 30s to write ONE sentence connecting them. Stories appear side by side. Algorithm picks the "better" one. But the real value is reading what the other person wrote — and laughing, or being impressed, or saying "wait, I almost wrote the same thing."

- Round: ~40s/rd | Build: Low
- *Share hook: side-by-side stories — "She wrote this in 30 seconds. I can't even."*

### 15. Gesture
Prompt: "Draw a star." Both have 15s to draw it. Both drawings appear side by side, framed like an art gallery. 2-second pause before winner announced. Shape recognition scores closeness. But the real winner is whoever made the other person laugh.

- Round: ~25s/rd | Build: Low
- *Share hook: side-by-side drawings — one perfect, one hilariously bad. "Guess which one is mine."*

### 16. Bouquet
8 flowers, each with color + shape. Both pick 3 to arrange. Both bouquets assemble in real-time on warm wood backgrounds. Algorithm scores: color harmony, variety, balance. Winner glows gently. Loser's bouquet is still beautiful. No harsh numbers.

- Round: ~20s/rd | Build: Low
- *Share hook: two bouquets side by side — "We both picked the rose. Great minds."*

### 17. Palette
A color swatch appears for 3s. Then a blank gradient. Both tap where the color was. Result: two markers on the gradient + the original color revealed as a glowing dot. Closest wins. Rounds get harder (teal vs mint, etc.). Gorgeous color transitions.

- Round: ~10s/rd | Build: Very Low
- *Share hook: the gradient reveal — "I was THIS close" with an arrow graphic*

### 18. Pairing
4×4 grid (8 pairs). Take turns flipping two cards. Find a pair → answer a question ("Best travel destination?" "Best meal this month?"). Opponent answers. First to 3 pairs wins. Post-game: full Q&A summary — a built-in conversation starter.

- Round: ~60s | Build: Low
- *Share hook: "We found 3 pairs and now I know their dream vacation. 10/10 would match again."*

## Strategy — "One wrong move"

### 19. Scales
Both secretly choose a weight (1-10). The balance scale tilts. Heavier wins. **Catch**: choose >7 AND be heavier → the scale BREAKS on your side, you lose the round. The scale wobbles, creaks, then settles or shatters. Best of 5.

- Round: ~8s/rd | Build: Very Low
- *Share hook: the scale breaking animation — brass plates scattering, the slow-motion crack*

### 20. Plank
Two figures on a plank over water. Each round: **advance** or **stand firm**. Both advance → clash (one gets pushed back). One advances, one stands → stander retreats. Both stand → standoff. 10 segments. Fall off = lose. The plank creaks, the water churns below, the camera zooms in on the last few steps.

- Round: ~30-50s | Build: Low-Medium
- *Share hook: the POV shot from behind the character on the last segment — heart in throat*

### 21. Sumo
Two circles in a shrinking ring. Tilt to lean, tap to push. Physics-based — momentum, release, stumble. Ring shrinks over time, forcing the end. Minimalist sumo wrestlers on a dohyo. Abstract crowd gasps.

- Round: ~20-40s | Build: Low
- *Share hook: the slow-motion near-stumble — "I THOUGHT I had them"*

### 22. Boil
Shared pot heats. Take turns adding one of 3 ingredients. Each shifts the boil-over threshold (hidden). Status displayed: "cool" → "warm" → "hot" → "about to boil". Whoever causes the boil-over loses. The final seconds — pot bubbling, steam rising — are excruciating.

- Round: ~40s | Build: Medium
- *Share hook: the boil-over animation — "I KNEW I shouldn't have added chili"*

### 23. Kite Fight
Top-down, two kites in a shared sky. Steer left/right. Cross the opponent's string and tug = cut = they lose. The string-angle meta-game: expose your line as bait, then dodge as they go for it. A kite falling in slow motion — tumbling, spiraling — is breathtaking.

- Round: ~30s | Build: Medium
- *Share hook: the kite falling slow-mo against a sunset sky — beautiful even in defeat*

## Knowledge / Timed

A family of quick-fire knowledge games sharing the same 5s-timer mechanic, but each with its own name, visual identity, and question bank. They feel like completely different games.

### 24. Food Remedy 🆕
**Visual identity:** Earth tones, food photography / illustration, botanical accents. A mortar and pestle logo. Warm greens and ambers.

Both see a health condition + 4 natural food options. 5 seconds to pick the best food remedy. Correct = 1pt. Correct + faster = 2pt. Wrong = 0pt. Best of 7.

Each answer reveals a "Did you know?" fact — the viral core: *"Which food is best for lowering blood pressure?"* → Beetroot (nitrates → vasodilation, works within hours). *"Best natural source of vitamin D?"* → Mushrooms (exposed to UV light). *"Which food helps with anxiety?"* → Dark chocolate (magnesium regulates cortisol).

The questions are designed so the answer is surprising but believable — the "wait, really?" reaction is what people screenshot and share.

The heartbeat: the 5s ring closing, the chime of correct, the "Did you know?" card sliding in with a botanical flourish.

- Match: ~60s | Build: Low (question bank + timer)
- *Share hook: "I got 7/7 on Food Remedy. Did you know beetroot lowers BP in 2 hours? 🫣"*

### 25. Style Match 🆕
**Visual identity:** Fashion editorial illustrations, color swatches, mood boards. A hanger/closet logo. Blush pinks, creams, navy.

An event/occasion appears with illustrated options: *"Beach wedding: which shoe?"* (with 4 shoe illustrations). *"Rooftop date sunset: what color top?"* (with 4 color swatches). *"Garden party: dress or jumpsuit?"* 5 seconds to pick the most appropriate option.

Each question is a mini fashion editorial — beautiful illustrations that are shareable on their own. The correct answer awards 1pt, faster correct = 2pt.

The heartbeat: the outfit animation — options appear as if on a runway, the 5s timer, the "correct" chime with a sparkle effect on the winning outfit.

- Match: ~60s | Build: Low-Medium (illustration assets)
- *Share hook: side-by-side question screenshot — "Which shoe for a beach wedding? I chose the wedge 🌊"*

### 26. Kitchen Math 🆕
**Visual identity:** Recipe card aesthetic, measuring cups, ingredients. A chef's knife logo. Warm whites, parchment tones, red accents.

A recipe scaling problem appears: *"Scaling from 6 to 10 servings. Original calls for 3 cups flour. How much?"* 5 seconds, 4 options. Correct = 1pt. Faster correct = 2pt.

Questions test real kitchen math: ingredient scaling, temperature conversions, time adjustments, unit substitutions. Satisfying when you nail it under pressure.

The heartbeat: the recipe card flipping to reveal the question, the 5s countdown, the "correct!" with a chef's kiss animation. Wrong answer shows the calculation broken down.

- Match: ~60s | Build: Low (question bank + recipe card UI)
- *Share hook: "I scored 100% on Kitchen Math and I've never cooked a day in my life"*

### 27. Ingredient Swap 🆕
**Visual identity:** Pantry shelf aesthetic, ingredient close-ups. A swap/arrow circular logo. Warm terracottas and olive greens.

"You're out of buttermilk. What can you substitute?" 4 options, 5 seconds. Some questions have ONE correct answer, others are trick questions where ALL work — those are the viral moments.

The trick questions are the engine: *"Out of buttermilk"* → everyone argues about milk + lemon juice vs yogurt vs sour cream → reveal: ALL WORK. *"No eggs for baking"* → mashed banana, applesauce, flax egg, yogurt → ALL WORK (but banana changes flavor). The partial-credit reveals ("banana works but changes the flavor profile") create debate.

The heartbeat: the moment all 4 options light up as correct on a trick question — the opponent's jaw drop, the "wait, REALLY?" reaction.

- Match: ~60s | Build: Low (question bank + ingredient UI)
- *Share hook: "ALL FOUR work as buttermilk substitutes. I've been buying buttermilk for NOTHING"*

### 28. 5-Move Chess ♟️
**Visual identity:** Classic chessboard, wooden pieces, evaluation bar. Minimal, premium. A knight piece logo.

Both players see the same chess position (handpicked endgame/middlegame). Each gets 5 moves total. A lightweight engine (Stockfish WASM) evaluates each move and shows the evaluation bar shifting in real-time. After 5 moves, the player with the higher final evaluation wins.

The tension: watching your opponent's eval bar climb while yours dips. The last move — do you go for the aggressive line or play safe? The engine feedback after each move (in the spectator view, also visible to the opponent in the rematch screen) creates the "I should have played..." post-game.

Positions are curated for drama — not trivial, not impossibly complex. The board state should have multiple plausible lines of play.

The heartbeat: the final move, the eval bar settling, the game declaring a winner. The post-game analysis showing where you went wrong.

- Match: ~60-90s | Build: High (needs Stockfish WASM integration)
- *Share hook: the evaluation bar comparison — "I was winning until move 4. ONE bad move cost me the game."*

## Visual / Observation

### 29. Spot the Difference
Two near-identical images side by side. First to tap a difference = 1pt. First to 3 wins. Themed packs rotate. The satisfaction of spotting it before the opponent is visceral.

- Round: ~15s/diff | Build: Medium
- *Share hook: the image pair itself is shareable — "Can you spot all 3?"*

### 30. War (Ante)
Both flip a card simultaneously. Higher wins both. **Twist**: antes. Before each flip, bet 1 or 3 cards from your pile. Win → take opponent's bet too. Lose → lose yours. Empty opponent's deck to win. The simultaneous reveal + the ante mechanic creates rollercoaster swings.

- Round: ~30s | Build: Low
- *Share hook: "I bet 3 with a 2 showing and pulled a king. My opponent's face."*
